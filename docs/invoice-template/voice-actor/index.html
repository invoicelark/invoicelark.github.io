Voice Actor Invoice Template

Voiceover pricing is usage pricing: the same 30 seconds is worth different money in a corporate training module versus a national ad campaign. Separate the session fee from the usage license on every invoice, and put pickups policy in the terms.

Typical voice actor rates

$150–$500 per finished minute for corporate; broadcast rates by market. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for voice actors

License renewals are the annuity of VO work — calendar the expiry dates from your invoices and send a friendly renewal invoice a month before each one.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every voice actor invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
