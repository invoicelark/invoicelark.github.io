Mobile Mechanic Invoice Template

Auto repair invoices carry a trust burden: the customer can't see what you did. Beat that with specificity — parts with brand and price, labor hours per job, and the diagnostic as its own line. Vehicle details (make, model, odometer) belong on every invoice.

Typical mobile mechanic rates

$70–$150/hour; mobile call-out $50–$100. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for mobile mechanics

Note the odometer reading and next-service recommendation on each invoice — it builds a service history that keeps customers coming back to you instead of the dealership.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every mobile mechanic invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
