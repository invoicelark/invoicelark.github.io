Plumber Invoice Template

Plumbing invoices are read by stressed people who just had water where it shouldn't be, so clarity wins: diagnostic fee, labor, parts with a disclosed markup, and any after-hours surcharge on separate lines. License number on every invoice — property managers can't pay without it.

Typical plumber rates

$75–$180/hour; call-out fees $90–$250. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for plumbers

Quote fixed prices for standard jobs (heater swap, faucet install) and hourly only for diagnosis-first work. Fixed quotes end price arguments and reward your speed.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every plumber invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
