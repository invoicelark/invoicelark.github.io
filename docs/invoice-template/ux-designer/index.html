UX/UI Designer Invoice Template

Product design work ships in phases — research, wireframes, high-fidelity, handoff — and invoices should bill the phase that was accepted, not the pixels produced. Scope is measured in screens and flows, so put those numbers on the line items.

Typical ux/ui designer rates

$60–$180/hour; app design projects $5,000–$50,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for ux/ui designers

Bill per accepted phase with sign-off before the next begins — a client who approved wireframes in writing can't relitigate them during high-fidelity.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every ux/ui designer invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
