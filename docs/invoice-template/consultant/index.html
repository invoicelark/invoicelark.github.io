Consultant Invoice Template

Consulting invoices carry more scrutiny than any other freelance invoice — often a finance team, not your contact, approves them. Reference the engagement or SOW number, break work into phases, and attach nothing that needs explaining.

Typical consultant rates

$100–$400/hour; day rates $1,000–$5,000 depending on field. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for consultants

Put the client's PO or SOW reference on the invoice — large-company accounts payable departments routinely sit on invoices that lack one, and it is never their job to chase you for it.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every consultant invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
