Massage Therapist Invoice Template

Therapists invoice two very different payers: individuals (simple, per-session, often prepaid packages) and businesses or insurers (who need session dates, durations, and credentials on paper). Keep one clean format that satisfies the strictest payer and use it everywhere.

Typical massage therapist rates

$60–$150 per hour-long session; corporate on-site $75–$120/hour. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for massage therapists

Put your certification/license number and session durations on every invoice — it's mandatory for insurance reimbursement clients and costs nothing for everyone else.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every massage therapist invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
