Hairstylist Invoice Template

Salon and freelance stylists bill a mix of services, products and time, and the cleanest invoices keep those three separate. For bridal and event work, the invoice also protects your date: deposit up front, balance before the big day, travel and early-start fees stated plainly.

Typical hairstylist rates

$40–$120/hour; bridal packages $200–$800. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for hairstylists

For weddings, invoice the trial separately and credit it against the package if they book — you get paid for the trial either way, and the credit feels like a discount.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every hairstylist invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
