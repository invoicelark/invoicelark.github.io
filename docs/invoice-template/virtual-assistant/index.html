Virtual Assistant Invoice Template

VA work is many small tasks, and an invoice that lists every one looks chaotic while an invoice that says "VA services" looks opaque. The fix is category lines with hour subtotals — inbox, scheduling, research, admin — backed by a time log you attach.

Typical virtual assistant rates

$25–$75/hour; monthly retainers $500–$2,500. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for virtual assistants

Sell hour-block retainers invoiced at the start of the month rather than loose hours billed after. Cash flow flips in your favor and clients treat reserved hours with more respect.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every virtual assistant invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
