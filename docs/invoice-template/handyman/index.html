Handyman Invoice Template

Handyman jobs are bundles of small tasks, so the invoice should read like the to-do list you cleared: each task its own line with a price. Clients tolerate a $480 total far better when they see six named jobs than one line that says "repairs."

Typical handyman rates

$40–$100/hour; half-day rates $200–$400. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for handymans

Have a minimum charge and print it on the invoice. It filters out the "just one tiny thing" jobs that cost you an hour of driving for twenty minutes of billing.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every handyman invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
