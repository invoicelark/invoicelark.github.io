Illustrator Invoice Template

Illustration pricing is licensing pricing. The same drawing is worth $200 as a blog spot and $20,000 on national packaging, so the invoice must say where, how long, and how exclusively the client may use the work — or you have silently sold everything for the price of anything.

Typical illustrator rates

$50–$200/hour; editorial spots $250–$1,500; covers $1,000–$5,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for illustrators

State the license on the invoice itself ("single editorial use, worldwide web, 1 year") — an invoice the client paid is the cleanest possible evidence of what they actually bought.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every illustrator invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
