Data Analyst Invoice Template

Analysis invoices should name the question answered, not the hours spent staring at a query. 'Churn driver analysis with recommendations' is a deliverable; '14 hours SQL' is an interrogation waiting to happen. Dashboards and their maintenance belong on separate lines.

Typical data analyst rates

$60–$160/hour; dashboard projects $2,000–$15,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for data analysts

Sell ad-hoc work in prepaid 10-hour blocks — 'quick questions' arrive daily, and a running block turns them from scope creep into a product.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every data analyst invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
