Landscaper / Gardener Invoice Template

Landscaping runs on recurring service, and the invoice structure should match: monthly maintenance as one predictable line, one-off projects (installs, cleanups) quoted and billed separately. Mixing them teaches clients to question the monthly rate every time you do extra work.

Typical landscaper / gardener rates

$50–$100/hour; monthly maintenance $150–$600. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for landscaper / gardeners

Photograph completed one-off jobs and attach the link to the invoice — for absent property owners, the photo is the deliverable and the invoice approves itself.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every landscaper / gardener invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
