Painter / Decorator Invoice Template

Painting quotes collapse when 'the walls' turns out to include ceilings, trim and a stubborn wallpaper layer. The invoice should mirror a room-by-room quote, with prep work and materials visible as their own lines so scope changes have somewhere to land.

Typical painter / decorator rates

$40–$90/hour; per-room flat rates $300–$1,200. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for painter / decorators

Bill color changes after work starts as a new line item, never absorb them — 'while you're at it' is the most expensive sentence in the trades.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every painter / decorator invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
