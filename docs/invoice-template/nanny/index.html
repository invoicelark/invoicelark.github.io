Nanny / Babysitter Invoice Template

Regular childcare billing works best as a weekly or fortnightly invoice with a simple hours log; families are juggling enough without surprise totals. Overtime, late pickups and holiday rates should be standing terms printed on every invoice, not conversations at the door.

Typical nanny / babysitter rates

$18–$40/hour; overnight $100–$250/night. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for nanny / babysitters

Invoice weekly with a visible hours log and keep a standing late-pickup rate — it converts an awkward doorstep moment into a neutral line item.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every nanny / babysitter invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
