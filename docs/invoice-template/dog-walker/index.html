Dog Walker / Pet Sitter Invoice Template

Pet care billing is high-frequency and small-ticket, which makes per-walk invoicing pure overhead. Weekly or monthly consolidated invoices with a simple service log (date, dog, duration) keep it professional without drowning you in paperwork.

Typical dog walker / pet sitter rates

$15–$40 per walk; overnights $50–$120; monthly packages $250–$800. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for dog walker / pet sitters

Bill monthly in advance for regulars and add a stated holiday surcharge — the demand spike is predictable, and clients accept it when it's printed policy rather than a surprise.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every dog walker / pet sitter invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
