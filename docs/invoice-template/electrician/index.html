Electrician Invoice Template

Trade invoices need two things office invoices don't: a parts/labor split and compliance details. List materials with a modest disclosed markup, labor by hours or fixed quote, and your license number on every invoice — commercial clients often can't pay without it.

Typical electrician rates

$60–$150/hour; call-out fees $75–$200. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for electricians

Photograph the completed work and reference it on the invoice. For property managers and landlords it doubles as their compliance record, which makes your invoice the easy one to approve.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every electrician invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
