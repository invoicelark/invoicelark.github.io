Caterer Invoice Template

Catering invoices price people and logistics: per-head for the menu, separate lines for staff, rentals, delivery and service charge. Final headcount changes are the classic dispute, so the invoice should state the guaranteed minimum you quoted against.

Typical caterer rates

$25–$150 per head depending on menu and service level. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for caterers

Invoice against a guaranteed minimum headcount set 7 days out — you buy and staff for that number, so the invoice should hold even if fewer plates go out.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every caterer invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
