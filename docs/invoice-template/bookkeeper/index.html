A bookkeeper's invoice is a work sample — if it isn't immaculate, why would anyone trust you with theirs? Fixed monthly packages tied to transaction volume invoice cleaner than hourly, and year-end or cleanup work belongs on separate project lines.

Typical bookkeeper rates

$30–$90/hour; monthly packages $300–$2,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for bookkeepers

Price by transaction volume bands rather than hours. Clients understand "up to 150 transactions" instantly, and your growing efficiency raises your effective rate instead of cutting your bill.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every bookkeeper invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
