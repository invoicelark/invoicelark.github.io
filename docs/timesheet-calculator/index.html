How hours are calculated

Daily hours = (end − start) − break. Overnight shifts are handled automatically: if the end time is earlier than the start time, the calculator assumes the shift crossed midnight. Weekly overtime kicks in after the threshold you set (40 hours is the U.S. federal default; adjust it for your rules), and overtime hours are paid at the multiplier you choose.

Frequently asked questions

Does it handle overnight shifts?

Yes — a 22:00 start with a 06:00 end counts as 8 hours (minus break), not negative time.

Is my timesheet saved?

It's saved in your own browser automatically, so you can fill it in day by day through the week. Use "Clear week" to start fresh.

How do I bill these hours to a client?

Take the weekly total to our free invoice generator — one line item with your hours and rate is all it takes. And if you're not sure your rate is right, check it with the hourly rate calculator.
