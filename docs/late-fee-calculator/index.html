Can I even charge a late fee?

In most places, yes — if your invoice or contract said so up front. A common approach is 1–1.5% per month on overdue balances. Some jurisdictions also give you a statutory right to interest on late commercial payments (for example, the EU late-payment rules and the UK's statutory 8% + base rate). Check the rules where you operate, and put your late-fee terms on every invoice — our free invoice generator has a notes field made for exactly that.

Frequently asked questions

What's a reasonable late fee?

1% to 2% per month is typical and rarely disputed. Higher than that can be legally unenforceable in some jurisdictions and can sour a client relationship that might otherwise recover.

Should I always charge it?

Many freelancers use the late fee as leverage rather than revenue: mention that it's accruing, then offer to waive it if payment arrives within a few days. You get paid faster and stay the good guy.

How do I prevent late payments in the first place?

Shorter payment terms (7–14 days instead of 30), a deposit up front, late-fee terms printed on the invoice, and making payment as easy as possible. Fewer steps means faster money.
