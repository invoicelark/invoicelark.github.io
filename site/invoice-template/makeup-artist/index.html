Makeup Artist Invoice Template

Event work runs on dates, so the invoice's real job is protecting yours: deposit to lock the date, balance due before the event, early-start and travel fees stated plainly. Brides don't dispute line items — they dispute surprises.

Typical makeup artist rates

$50–$150 per face; bridal packages $250–$800. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for makeup artists

Make the deposit non-refundable and say so on the invoice — you are selling a date more than a service, and a cancelled Saturday in wedding season cannot be resold on Thursday.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every makeup artist invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
