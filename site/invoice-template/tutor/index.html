Tutor Invoice Template

Tutoring invoices go to parents who are comparing you with an agency, so look the part: student name, subject, session log with dates, and a monthly total. Billing monthly in advance for a fixed weekly slot beats chasing payment session by session.

Typical tutor rates

$25–$120/hour depending on level and subject. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for tutors

Invoice the month ahead, not behind, and offer a small sibling or prepay discount — parents value predictability and you eliminate the no-show problem financially.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every tutor invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
