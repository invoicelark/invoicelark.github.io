Event Planner Invoice Template

Event invoices must keep two rivers separate: your fee, and the client's money that merely flows through you to vendors. Itemize vendor payments distinctly (or better, have vendors bill the client directly) and stage your fee across planning milestones.

Typical event planner rates

10–20% of event budget, or $75–$200/hour; day-of coordination $800–$3,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for event planners

Invoice pass-through vendor costs separately and label them as such — mixing them into your fee inflates your apparent price and your tax reporting in one bad move.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every event planner invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
