Web Developer Invoice Template

Development invoices go wrong when they list hours against vague labels like "site work." Clients pay faster when each line maps to something they can see: a page, a feature, a fix. Group small tickets into one line with a short summary rather than thirty micro-entries.

Typical web developer rates

$50–$180/hour; small business sites typically $2,000–$15,000. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for web developers

Invoice per milestone (design approved / staging live / launch), never all-on-delivery. A site that is 90% done has 0% leverage if the full amount is still unbilled.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every web developer invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
