Cleaning Professional Invoice Template

Cleaning invoices are simple, and that is their power: date, location, service, price. The businesses that get paid fastest attach before/after photo links for one-off jobs and invoice recurring clients on a fixed monthly schedule rather than per visit.

Typical cleaning professional rates

$30–$80/hour; flat rates $120–$400 per home visit. Rates vary by market and experience — if you're unsure where you sit, run your numbers through our hourly rate calculator.

Billing tip for cleaning professionals

Move weekly clients to one monthly invoice via auto-payment. You send 12 invoices a year instead of 52, and the money arrives before the month's work instead of after each visit.

Tired of chasing payments? Put your payment terms and a late-fee clause on every invoice (our late fee calculator shows what an overdue balance actually costs your client), and make paying you as frictionless as possible.

What every cleaning professional invoice needs

  1. A unique invoice number and issue date
  2. Your business name, contact details (and tax/license ID where required)
  3. The client's name and billing details
  4. Clear line items — like the examples above — with quantities and rates
  5. Subtotal, any tax, and the total due
  6. Payment terms: due date, accepted methods, and your late-fee policy
