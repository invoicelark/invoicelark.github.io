Freelance Hourly Rate Calculator

“Take your old salary and divide by 2,000” is how freelancers end up underpaid. This calculator works backwards from the income you want, and accounts for the hours you can't bill, the costs a salary used to hide, and time off.

Costs = software, hardware, insurance, accounting, co-working, marketing, health cover — everything an employer used to pay for.

How the calculation works

The formula is straightforward but honest:

(Target income + business costs) ÷ billable hours per year × safety margin

Billable hours = (52 − weeks off) × hours per week × billable %. The safety margin covers late payers, dry months, and scope creep — things every freelancer meets eventually.

Frequently asked questions

Why is the rate so much higher than my old salary suggests?

Because as a freelancer you pay for everything an employer used to cover — equipment, insurance, retirement, sick days, slow weeks — and you can only bill a fraction of your working hours. A rate that "matches" your old salary is actually a large pay cut.

Should I charge hourly or per project?

Use your hourly rate as an internal floor, then quote per project where you can. Project pricing rewards you for being fast and experienced; hourly pricing punishes it. Our project quote calculator helps you build a project price from your rate.

What if the result is above the market rate for my field?

That's useful information: it means at market rates you'd need more billable hours, lower costs, or a different positioning (higher-value clients, productized services). Better to learn it from a calculator than from your bank balance.
